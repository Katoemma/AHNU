Child Protection and Advocacy: A Shield of Safety

The Problem: Children with Disabilities are Vulnerable to Abuse in Uganda

Uganda faces many challenges in protecting its children from abuse, especially those with disabilities. According to UNICEF, 75% of children in Uganda experience some form of violence before they reach 18 years old. Children with disabilities are more likely to be abused than their peers without disabilities, due to factors such as stigma, discrimination, isolation, dependency, and invisibility. The abuse can take various forms, such as physical, sexual, emotional, or neglect. The consequences of abuse can be devastating for children with disabilities, affecting their health, education, development, and well-being.

Despite the existence of laws and policies that aim to protect children from abuse, there are many gaps and challenges in their implementation and enforcement. Some of these challenges include lack of awareness, reporting, investigation, prosecution, and support services for victims and survivors. There is also a need for more coordination and collaboration among different actors and sectors involved in child protection.

But we firmly believe that every child has the right to live in safety and dignity.

The Solution: A Shared Responsibility: Engaging Communities for Child Safety

We have successfully facilitated over 30 community dialogues for discussions about shared responsibilities in safeguarding children. Our conversations span from heart-to-heart exchanges to practical steps that can turn a community into a fortress of protection. We’ve walked hand-in-hand with these communities, nurturing a profound understanding of the vital role they play in fostering a protective haven.

We also empower the local leaders and authorities who are responsible for ensuring the safety and welfare of the children in their areas. We’ve trained over 100 local community leaders in child protection, with an unwavering focus on children with disabilities. These local champions stand as guardians of innocence, armed with knowledge, awareness, and a deep commitment to create safe spaces. They serve as beacons of advocacy, ensuring that every child’s rights are upheld and their vulnerabilities are shielded.

How You Can Help: Join Us in Crafting a Protective Haven

Safeguarding children with disabilities requires not just the strength of conviction, but also the power of unity. You have the opportunity to make a profound impact:

  • Support: Contribute to our child protection initiatives and help create safer environments.
  • Amplify Awareness: Advocate for the rights and safety of children with disabilities, raising awareness in your circles.
  • Volunteer: Offer your time and expertise to strengthen our child protection efforts, from community engagement to advocacy.
  • Spread the Call: Share our mission with others, expanding the reach of our protective embrace.
