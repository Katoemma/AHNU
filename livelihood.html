Strong Hands: Providing Livelihood and Financial Literacy Support to People with Disabilities in Uganda

The Problem: People with Disabilities Face Poverty and Exclusion in Uganda

Uganda is a beautiful country, but it also faces many challenges in providing economic opportunities for all its people, especially those with disabilities. According to UNFPA, there are an estimated 12.4 million people with disabilities in Uganda, representing 12% of the total population. They include people with physical, sensory, mental, or intellectual impairments. Many of these people face poverty and exclusion in accessing livelihood and financial literacy support, such as:

  • Lack of skills and resources...
  • Lack of access to credit...
  • Lack of financial literacy...

The Solution: Providing Livelihood and Financial Literacy Support to People with Disabilities in Uganda

Our Strong Hands program extends a hand of support to people with disabilities in Uganda making sure that they have access to livelihood and financial literacy support in ways they can best access and understand.

The Progress: Improving the Livelihoods and Financial Well-being of People with Disabilities in Uganda

Our Strong Hands program is still at a pilot stage, but we have already seen some promising results. From the beginning of 2023, we have supported 21 enterprises in the first year of the project.
